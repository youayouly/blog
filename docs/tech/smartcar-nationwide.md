# National Intelligent Car Competition

The **National Undergraduate Intelligent Car Competition** (often associated with the **NXP Cup**) is a major engineering contest in China for autonomous model vehicles. It is organized under the guidance of the Ministry of Education’s automation teaching committee. Teams design cars with microcontrollers, sensors, and motor drivers to follow tracks with minimal human intervention.

## Tracks covered in one place

This page groups work from multiple official categories, including **full model** (完全模型组) and **vision** (视觉组) style challenges: embedded control, image processing for lane or marker detection, real-time control loops, and on-track tuning.

## Highlights

- Interdisciplinary mix of control theory, embedded C, computer vision, and mechanics.
- Regional qualifiers and a national final; scale reaches hundreds of universities.
- Typical stack: MCU (e.g. NXP), camera or specialized sensors, PID and state machines, optional ML on edge devices.
